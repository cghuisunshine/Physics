1.1 Measuring Things, Including Lengths

Physics starts with quantities you can measure and compare. Always include units, and be comfortable converting between unit systems and interpreting orders of magnitude.

SI base units (most used)

Quantity Unit Symbol
Length meter m
Time second s
Mass kilogram kg

Derived units: N = kg·m/s², J = N·m, W = J/s, Pa = N/m².

1.2 Time

Time measurement depends on stable periodic processes (atomic transitions, quartz oscillations, etc.). In problem solving, treat time as a base dimension with unit seconds.

1.3 Mass

Mass measures inertia (resistance to acceleration) and is distinct from weight (force due to gravity). Keep kg (mass) separate from N (force).

Weight: W = mg
Worked example: unit conversion habit

Convert grams to kilograms by dividing by 1000. Always convert to SI early to avoid mixed-unit mistakes.

Study tip: write units at every step. Dimensional analysis catches many errors quickly, especially when you’re unsure which formula to use.
