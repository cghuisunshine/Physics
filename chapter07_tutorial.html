7.1 Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy measures energy associated with motion. It depends on speed, not direction.

K = (1/2)mv2

7.2 Work and Kinetic Energy

Work is energy transferred by a force acting through a displacement. Net work equals the change in kinetic energy.

W = ∫ F · dr
Wnet = ΔK

For constant F along displacement: W = Fd cos θ.

7.3 Work Done by the Gravitational Force

Gravity is conservative near Earth: work depends only on the change in height, not the path.

Wg = −ΔUg = mg(yi − yf)

7.4 Work Done by a Spring Force

Spring force is variable: F = −kx. Work done by the spring is related to the change in spring potential energy.

Us = (1/2)kx2
Ws = −ΔUs

7.5 Work Done by a General Variable Force

For variable forces, work is the area under the F vs x curve (in 1D) or the line integral in multiple dimensions.

W = ∫xixf F(x) dx
Worked example: piecewise force graph

Compute work by summing geometric areas (rectangles/triangles) under the curve with the correct sign.

7.6 Power

Power is the rate of doing work (energy transfer per time). Instantaneous power is force dotted with velocity.

P̄ = W/Δt
P = dW/dt = F · v

Study tip: when forces vary or multiple forces act, use Wnet = ΔK. For conservative forces, translate work into a potential-energy change to simplify.
