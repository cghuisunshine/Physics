3.1 Vectors and Their Components

Vectors have magnitude and direction. Components express a vector in a coordinate basis, making addition/subtraction and kinematics much easier.

Components (2D)

A = (Ax, Ay)
|A| = √(Ax2 + Ay2)

3.2 Unit Vectors, Adding Vectors by Components

Unit vectors give direction only and form a convenient basis: î, ĵ, k̂. Add vectors by adding components.

A = Ax î + Ay ĵ (+ Az k̂)
A + B = (Ax + Bx)î + (Ay + By)ĵ

3.3 Multiplying Vectors

The dot product relates to projection and work; the cross product relates to torque and angular momentum. They encode different geometric relationships.

Dot product

A · B = |A||B| cos θ
A · B = AxBx + AyBy + AzBz

Cross product

|A × B| = |A||B| sin θ

Direction is given by the right-hand rule.

Study tip: do vector problems in components early. Save “magnitude/angle” for the final answer unless a diagram demands it.
